 Archaeological Museum

The National Archaeological Museum is the largest museum in the country 
and one of the most important in the world. Features more than 11,000 
exhibits, which depict the history of the ancient Greek civilization 
and other civilizations that passed through the Greek area. Like all 
the big museums are organized into sections, some of which 
are: prehistoric collection, collection of sculptures, collection jars and 
other small, findings of Santorini collection metallurgy, Egyptian art 
collection, antiques collection of the Near East. The museum was 
originally called "Central Archaeological Museum 'housed in the Temple 
of Hephaestus until 1874 where he moved to his present location. 
The south wing of the museum houses the Epigraphic Museum, which has the 
richest collection of inscriptions worldwide.
