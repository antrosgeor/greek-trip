Aristotle Square

The Aristotle Square is the largest square in Thessaloniki and one of 
the largest and most impressive in Greece. Its current form was formed 
after the great fire of 1917 by French architect Ernest Hebrard. Upon 
Aristotle are some of the most important and beautiful buildings in the 
city such as the Olympian, which conducted the Thessaloniki Film Festival 
and the twin building of Hotel Electra Palace. Some of the best bookstores 
in the city are in the square as well as several radio stations. 
All important events celebrated in Aristotle Square (
Christmas, Pancake, etc.).
